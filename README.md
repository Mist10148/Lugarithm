## Story

You play as a young man who has just lost his father — someone he barely knew. His father left behind two things: a vintage jeepney kept in perfect running order, and a thick journal meant to explain the family's history. When he finally opens it, every page has been torn out (*gisi*). A note inside says the pages were given away to old friends in towns along the coast, one per town, so the son would have to go meet them himself.

So he drives.

The game follows a single road trip down the old coastal highway from **Iloilo City to San Joaquin**, stopping at five historic towns to find the person holding a page and earn it back. Each stop is a real place with real history. By the time the player reaches the final town, they've absorbed enough to give the tour themselves — which is the whole point.

The entire coastal route acts as an **interactive living museum** — heritage is never delivered on a menu screen. You arrive in a town, step out of the jeepney, and handle what's in front of you.

## Core Gameplay

### The Drive
The drive plays out like a moving painting — a 2D coastal scene scrolling by while your passenger talks. Your job is keeping the old jeepney from falling apart. When something breaks down (engine overheating, a belt snapping, lights cutting out), a **Repair Puzzle** kicks in.

### Repair Puzzles — Two Difficulty Modes
Accessible from **Settings** at any time:

- **Easy — Block Mode:** Arrange visual instruction blocks in the correct order to fix the breakdown. Drag-and-drop logic, no syntax required.
- **Hard — Code Mode:** Write the actual repair logic in code. Same problems, real programming syntax, higher rewards.

Both modes teach the same computational thinking — the difficulty setting just changes the interface.

### Town Puzzles
Each town has a unique puzzle tied to its actual heritage (e.g., assembling the pre-colonial Oton Gold Mask, restoring a damaged church facade). Solving these earns a **journal page** and advances the story.

You can't truly "lose" — mistakes only reduce your earnings at the end of a leg. A first run takes around **3–5 hours**.

## Level Variety

Levels aren't all structured the same way. Each town introduces a different mode of experiencing its heritage:

| Level Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Passenger as Guide** | A local passenger rides with you and shares stories during the drive. They assist with the upcoming town's puzzle. History arrives through conversation. |
| **You as Tour Guide** | You take tourists through the town yourself, explaining its heritage as you walk them through it. Arriving at a landmark rewards you with an artifact and its cultural context. |
| **Special Challenges** | Unique puzzle formats and heritage encounters unlocked through progression or high scores. |

The mix ensures the coast never feels like a repeated loop — each town has its own voice and its own way of handing history to the player.

---

## Passengers

Each town introduces a specific passenger who knew the father personally. They ride with the player from one town to the next, sharing stories and actively helping solve the upcoming puzzle. Their presence is what makes the history feel **inherited rather than assigned**.

## AI Features

Three AI systems are built into the game:

### Dynamic Passenger Dialogue
Passengers don't repeat the same lines. When you interact with them, their responses are freshly generated each playthrough — staying true to their character and the real history — so conversations feel alive on repeat runs.

### The Almanac & AI Journal Assistant
Recovered journal pages compile into an in-game **Almanac of Panay history**. An integrated AI chatbot lets you:
- Ask historical questions about what you've unlocked
- Request contextual hints if you're stuck on a puzzle

It only answers from pages you've actually collected — it never misleads you with information you haven't earned yet.

### Post-Level Analytics
After every puzzle or repair, an analytics screen breaks down your performance:
- How efficiently you solved it vs. the optimal solution
- A step-by-step walkthrough of the fastest possible approach
- Your score and currency earned

This encourages replaying for higher scores and more gacha currency.

## The Journal

The journal is the heart of the game. Each page recovered from each town adds a piece of a single connected story — from pre-colonial gold-working and burial customs through the Spanish colonial churches that doubled as forts, to the local craftsmen who worked their own marks into the stone. The final town closes the personal side of the story by explaining why the father scattered the pages in the first place.
